Introduction

Keeping your Honda’s GPS navigation system up to date is crucial for ensuring accurate directions, accessing the latest points of interest, and enjoying improved features and performance. Whether you're commuting daily or heading on a road trip, an updated GPS ensures you get where you're going efficiently and safely.

This comprehensive guide will walk you through the full process of updating your Honda GPS system. You'll learn what tools are needed, how to check for updates, and how to install them. The guide is designed for all levels, whether you're a tech-savvy user or a complete beginner.

Step 1: Know Your Honda Model

Honda vehicles use different navigation systems depending on the year and model. It’s important to identify your specific model and head unit (such as Garmin, HERE, or HondaLink) to ensure you get the correct update.

  • Check your vehicle manual for the GPS system type.
  • Alternatively, go to your infotainment screen → Settings → System Info.

Step 2: Gather the Tools

Before starting, ensure you have the following:

  • A computer with internet access (Windows or macOS)
  • A USB drive with at least 16GB of free space (FAT32 formatted)
  • Your Honda vehicle with the ignition turned on

Step 3: Download Honda Navigation Tool

Honda partners with navigation providers like Garmin and HERE to manage map updates. Depending on your system:

Download the respective software and install it on your PC or Mac.

Step 4: Insert USB and Collect Vehicle Info

Insert the empty USB drive into your Honda's infotainment system. Navigate to the map or system settings, and choose the option to export vehicle information. This process copies a unique file to the USB which is required for downloading the correct update.

Step 5: Download Map Update

Remove the USB from your car and insert it into your computer. Open the navigation tool (Garmin Express or NaviExtras Toolbox). The software will detect your system and show if an update is available.

  • If free updates are available, you can proceed immediately.
  • Otherwise, you'll be offered a purchase option for a paid update.

Download the update onto the USB drive. This can take some time depending on your internet speed.

Step 6: Install Update in Vehicle

Safely eject the USB from your computer and reinsert it into your car. Your infotainment system should detect the update and prompt you to install. Confirm and begin the installation.

The update may take 20–60 minutes depending on file size. Do not turn off the ignition during the process.

Why It's Important to Keep Your GPS Updated

Navigation systems are only as reliable as their data. Roads change constantly—new construction, traffic patterns, and closed routes can cause outdated systems to misguide you.

  • Access new and modified roads
  • Updated POIs: gas stations, ATMs, restaurants
  • Better routing and real-time traffic integration
  • System bug fixes and performance enhancements

FAQs

  • Are updates free? Many Honda systems include one or two free updates within a limited period. Additional updates may require purchase.
  • How often should I update? Ideally once a year or before long trips.
  • Can I update using my phone? No. Updates require a computer with internet and a USB drive.

Need Help?

If you run into issues during the update process, consult your owner's manual or visit Honda Support for assistance.
